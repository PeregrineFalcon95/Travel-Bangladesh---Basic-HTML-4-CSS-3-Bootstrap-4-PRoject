Bangladesh Parliament Dhaka

Bangladesh National Parliament is one of the must-sees of Bangladesh, an architectural masterpiece and a real accomplishment for the country. It was designed by world-renowned American architect and Yale University Professor "Louis I. Kahn".

Tea Garden Sylhet

The opulent green tea plantation attracts most tourists. It has overall 163 tea plantations. The tea of highest quality is produced here. Sylhet is the place where tea and tea gardens are available from where Sylhet has been contributing for improving economic growth of Bangladesh.

Sajek Valley

Sajek valley is famous for its natural beauty. Sajek valley is known as the Queen of Hills & Roof of Rangamati. The valley is surrounded by mountains, dense forest, grasslands hilly tracks. Many small rivers flow through the mountains among which Kachalong and Machalong are notable.

Saint Martin island

Saint Martin Bangladesh, in the Chittagong Division, is the only coral island of the country. ... Somebody named it “Daruchini Dwip” for the surrounding naturally beautiful scenery and crystal clear blue water of the Bay of Bengal. Unlike the long sandy beach of Cox's Bazar, you can walk around this entire small island.

Sundarbans

Sundarban is the world biggest mangrove forest. In Bangladesh tourism, Sunderban plays the most vital role. A large number of foreigners come to Bangladesh every year only to visit this unique mangrove forest. Besides, local tourists also go to visit Sundarban every year.

Cox's Bajar

Bangladeshis are extremely friendly, helpful and peace-loving people. Driving along the Marine Drive which stretches throughout the 120 km long sandy sea beach is one of the most exciting ways to explore Cox's Bazar. You can drive down south of the city to Himchari beach and further down to Inani beach.
